Title: SevaGrid Command Premium Product Showcase
Target duration: 5 to 8 minutes
Voice direction: warm, confident, professional female presenter; slower than a typical AI voiceover; short pauses between major sections.

Introduction

During Mahakumbh, volunteer coordination is not a normal admin problem. It is a live operational problem happening across crowded ghats, camps, transit corridors, medical points, helpdesks, and command sectors at the same time.

Thousands of volunteers may be available, but the real challenge is deciding who should move, where they should go, and why that is the safest decision right now.

SevaGrid Command is designed for that moment. It is a real-time volunteer operations command center that helps teams manage skills, location, workload, incidents, and coverage from one map-first workspace.

Product Overview

The first thing to notice is that this is not a traditional dashboard. There are no generic KPI cards fighting for attention. The map is the primary surface, because in field operations, every decision belongs to a place.

Each sector node carries operational meaning. The commander can see the sector name, risk score, coverage percentage, and deployment status directly on the map. Route lines show how volunteers are being moved across the operation, while incident beacons stay attached to the sector where support is needed.

This creates a command-center experience. The team is not just reading numbers. They are watching the operation as a spatial system.

Feature Walkthrough: Scenario Modes

At the top, the compact command bar controls the operating mode. Balanced mode gives a normal staffing picture. Snan Surge increases demand near ghats and transit corridors. Heat Alert shifts the plan toward medical aid, water relief, and fatigue-sensitive assignments. Missing Person mode prioritizes child support, language support, and helpdesk coverage.

These modes matter because Mahakumbh conditions change quickly. A static roster cannot respond to crowd pressure, heat, or a missing-person escalation. SevaGrid lets the operator simulate that pressure and immediately see how staffing demand changes.

Feature Walkthrough: Skill Filtering And Pressure Controls

The skill filter lets the operator focus on a specific capability. For example, they can inspect medical aid, crowd guidance, language support, logistics, security liaison, child support, or water relief.

This is important because volunteer availability alone is not enough. A high-risk sector may need the right skill at the right time, not just more people.

Crowd and heat sliders add another layer of realism. As pressure increases, the optimizer recalculates demand, target staffing, risk, and coverage. The interface is designed to make those changes visible without overwhelming the operator.

Feature Walkthrough: AI Dispatch Recommendations

The right side of the interface is the AI Dispatch panel. This panel explains who should move and why.

Recommendations are ranked using multiple operational signals: skill fit, current location, nearby sector coverage, language coverage, mobility preference, incident match, current workload, fatigue, and fairness.

That last part is critical. A deployment system should not simply assign the closest volunteer every time. It should close the highest-risk gap while protecting exhausted volunteers and keeping the workforce sustainable across the shift.

Feature Walkthrough: Incident Response

Now watch the incident workflow. When a new incident is added, it enters the live incident queue and becomes spatially connected to the affected sector. The related risk and demand change, and the assignment recommendations update around the new operational requirement.

The operator can send a response ticket from the incident queue without leaving the command surface. That keeps the workflow fast: detect, understand, assign, and respond.

Feature Walkthrough: Volunteers And Activity

The volunteer suggestions view shows recommended responders and the reserve pool. This helps commanders balance immediate response with workforce health.

The bottom activity layer records recent deployments and system events. It gives the command team a lightweight operational trail without blocking the map.

Navigation And Interaction Design

The navigation rail stays collapsed to protect the map. It expands only when needed. Keyboard shortcuts also support fast operation: number keys select priority zones, bracket keys cycle scenarios, A adds an incident, and T toggles the activity layer.

The design goal is minimal chrome and maximum operational focus.

Responsive Experience

The large-screen version is the primary command center. It is meant for control rooms and operations desks. But the product also adapts to tablet and mobile views for field review.

On tablet, the command surface keeps the same hierarchy while giving operators a readable map, controls, and operations panel.

On mobile, the layout is stabilized for supervisors who need to inspect status from smaller screens. The map remains usable, controls wrap cleanly, and there is no broken horizontal overflow.

Technical Highlights

The project is built with Next.js, React, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, Framer Motion, and Vercel.

The current demo uses typed local sample data, which makes it easy for judges to inspect the behavior without credentials, API keys, or private infrastructure.

The optimization engine is deterministic and explainable. It calculates demand, risk, target staffing, volunteer fit, missing skills, bench lists, protected volunteers, coverage, fairness, fatigue, and critical gaps.

Security And Validation

The deployed app is production-aware. It uses HTTPS on Vercel, includes baseline security headers, contains no backend secrets, collects no real personal data, and uses synthetic sample data for the demo.

Validation is documented in the repository. Lint passes, the production build passes, TypeScript validation passes through the build, and dependency audit reports zero vulnerabilities.

Theme Fit

For the hackathon theme, SevaGrid covers the full workflow. It models volunteer profiles, manages availability and fatigue, allocates responders to operational sectors, monitors incidents spatially, and optimizes deployment based on skills, location, workload, and operational requirements.

Roadmap And Closing

The roadmap is practical: import volunteer signup data, add GIS route timing, connect WhatsApp or SMS dispatch, support supervisor acknowledgement, add authenticated roles, track live check-ins, and forecast incidents from crowd and weather history.

SevaGrid Command shows how volunteer coordination at Mahakumbh can become faster, safer, and more explainable.

It is not just a roster. It is an operating system for real-time volunteer deployment.
